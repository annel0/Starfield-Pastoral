# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Players using StardewCraft in Minecraft, often inside a live multiplayer session. They need in-game civic tools that feel like Stardew Valley, stay readable at different GUI scales, and let them complete transactions without guessing where to click.

## Product Purpose
StardewCraft brings Stardew Valley-style farming, social, economy, and town systems into Minecraft. UI succeeds when it feels diegetic, warm, and handcrafted while still being precise enough for real multiplayer workflows like money transfers, farm cancellation, and auctions.

## Brand Personality
Warm, pastoral, meticulous. The interface should feel like town-office paperwork, market ledgers, wood signs, stamped forms, and festival booths rather than a generic admin panel.

## Anti-references
Avoid black admin dashboards, white text on bright parchment, cramped form rows, flat rectangular layouts, unclear focus states, decorative shadows behind text, and controls that look ornamental but do not explain their state.

## Design Principles
Make the next action obvious through layout, not explanation.
Use Stardew materials first: parchment panels, ledger lines, item slots, gold coin icons, warm wood accents.
Separate decision bands: item selection, schedule, pricing, and final action should not compete.
Every input must visibly show hover, focus, disabled, selected, and invalid states.
Delight belongs in feedback: small glints, pulsing focus, soft selection bands, and useful sounds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Prioritize high contrast text on warm surfaces, avoid text shadows, support reduced visual clutter at small GUI scale, and keep every clickable hitbox larger than the visible text label.
